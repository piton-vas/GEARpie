STRATEGY FOR POM-C ADAPTATION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

POM-C provides 40% higher contact stress limit (35 vs 25 MPa) and 62% higher
bending stress limit (26 vs 16 MPa) compared to PA6_PRINT. This allows:

1. Using same tooth geometry with higher safety margins
2. Using smaller modules while maintaining equivalent stress
3. Achieving higher ratios with better reliability

MATERIAL BENEFITS SUMMARY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

With POM_C sun + PA6_PRINT satellites vs PA6_PRINT throughout:

  Contact stress: +40% capacity
    └─ Allows i=32 designs with safety ≥ 1.3 (vs marginal with PA6)

  Bending stress: +62% capacity
    └─ Eliminates root-break risk on high-load stages
    └─ Enables smaller modules for compactness

  Machinability: Excellent (POM-C vs cast)
    └─ CNC-friendly for sun and internal gears
    └─ Tolerance: ±0.05 mm achievable
    └─ No heat treatment required

  Cost-effective:
    └─ POM-C stock material (mm sheets, rods)
    └─ Standard CNC operations
    └─ No special tooling


MECHANICAL ADVANTAGES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

POM-C Properties:
  ├─ E = 3000 MPa (between steel and PA6)
  ├─ Self-lubricating (low friction coefficient)
  ├─ Creep-resistant to 80°C
  ├─ Excellent dimensional stability
  └─ Low moisture absorption

Combined with PA6 satellites:
  ├─ Hybrid system balances rigidity (POM sun) with damping (PA6)
  ├─ Lower contact stress due to moduli difference
  └─ Better load distribution at tooth flanks


VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Before manufacturing, verify each design with:

1. Full VDI 2736 calculation (included in GEARpie)
2. Flank pressure: σH with proper flash temperature check
3. Root bending: σF with stress concentration factors
4. Planetary clearance: min 0.5×m between satellite edges
5. Assembly geometry: (Z_sun + Z_ring) / n_sat = integer
6. Dynamic load: Account for impact factors (KA)
7. Temperature analysis: No exceeding 80°C in sustained operation
