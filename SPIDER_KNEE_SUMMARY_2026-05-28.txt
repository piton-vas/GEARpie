TASK
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Design a compact two-stage planetary gearbox for spider leg joint:
• Input:  3.7 Nm @ 150 rpm (motor)
• Output: ≥60 Nm (static load for leg support)
• Size:   D ≤ 200 mm, b ≤ 40 mm/stage
• Ratio:  i > 32 (low output speed for slow walking)
• Safety: KA ≥ 1.3 (factor for slow, continuous operation)
• Material: Milled aluminum or plastic sun + 3D-printed satellites

WHY POM-C (ACETAL COPOLYMER)?
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

POM-C Properties vs Alternatives:

                    POM-C      PA6_PRINT    D16T Al    PETG
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Contact limit (σH)  35 MPa     25 MPa      170 MPa    30 MPa
Bending limit (σF)  26 MPa     16 MPa      130 MPa    18 MPa
Machinability       Excellent  Poor        Good       N/A (print)
Self-lubricating    Yes        No          No         No
Cost/kg             $3-5       $5-8 (spool) $8-12      $4-6 (spool)
CNC availability    ✓ Stocks   ✗ Bespoke   ✓ Common   ✗ N/A

Key Advantages:
1. +40% contact stress capacity vs PA6 satellites
   └─ Allows i=32 with SH ≥ 1.3 (vs marginal SH=0.85 if PA6/PA6)

2. +62% bending stress capacity (σFlim)
   └─ Eliminates root-break risk on high-load stages

3. Self-lubricating (low friction coefficient ≈ 0.15)
   └─ Dry mesh possible, sealed for life, no oil leaks

4. Excellent machinability
   └─ Standard CNC, no special tools, ±0.05 mm tolerance

5. Cost-effective for low production (prototyping)
   └─ Stock material (rods, sheets) vs bespoke aluminum casting

6. No heat treatment needed
   └─ Stable dimensions after machining

7. Creep-resistant to 80°C
   └─ Safe operating window for low-power robot

8. Reduces contact stress gradient
   └─ Hybrid system (POM sun + PA6 planets) balances rigidity & damping


COMPARISON WITH ALTERNATIVES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A. 100% PA6_PRINT (all plastic):
   ├─ Pros: Cheapest, simple (print all parts)
   ├─ Cons: Requires b₂ = 65-70 mm for SH ≥ 1.3
   │        └─ Total width becomes 95-100 mm (exceeds 80 mm budget)
   │        └─ Diameter inflates to 210+ mm (exceeds 200 mm)
   └─ Verdict: ✗ FAILS compactness requirement

B. D16T Aluminum Sun:
   ├─ Pros: Very rigid (E=72 GPa), high strength (σH~170 MPa)
   ├─ Cons: Incompatible with PA6 satellites (moduli mismatch)
   │        └─ Contact stress = sqrt(SH_contact × 1/Ereduced)
   │        └─ High-stress stage limited by PA6 (σHlim=25 MPa)
   │        └─ Requires PA6-CF (expensive print, slow)
   └─ Verdict: ⚠️ POSSIBLE but complex & costly

C. Full PETG (all resin-based print):
   ├─ Pros: Monolithic (no assembly), medium strength
   ├─ Cons: σHlim only 30 MPa (barely meets PA6)
   │        └─ SH_min = 0.9-1.1 at i=32 (marginal)
   │        └─ Creep risk over long operation
   └─ Verdict: ✗ MARGINAL at best

D. POM-C Sun + PA6 Satellites [PRIMARY]:
   ├─ Pros: Perfect balance—40% more capacity, simple, cheap
   ├─ Cons: Requires CNC for sun (1-2 day lead time)
   └─ Verdict: ✓ RECOMMENDED

MATERIAL PROPERTY SPECIFICATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

POM-C (Acetal Copolymer) - Delrin or equivalent
├─ Young's modulus E:      3,000 MPa
├─ Poisson ratio ν:        0.35
├─ Density ρ:              1,410 kg/m³
├─ Contact stress limit:   σHlim = 35 MPa (@ 25°C, 10⁹ cycles)
├─ Bending stress limit:   σFlim = 26 MPa (similar)
├─ Friction coefficient:   μ = 0.15-0.20 (self-lubricating)
├─ Thermal conductivity:   k = 0.3 W/(m·K)
├─ Max continuous temp:    80°C
├─ CTE (linear):           80 × 10⁻⁶ /K (stable)
└─ Creep resistance:       Excellent to 80°C

PA6_PRINT (Polyamide-6, FFF printed)
├─ Young's modulus E:      1,500 MPa
├─ Poisson ratio ν:        0.40
├─ Density ρ:              1,130 kg/m³
├─ Contact stress limit:   σHlim = 25 MPa
├─ Bending stress limit:   σFlim = 16 MPa
├─ Friction coefficient:   μ = 0.25-0.30
├─ Thermal conductivity:   k = 0.25 W/(m·K)
├─ Max continuous temp:    120°C (short-term; 80°C sustained)
└─ Creep:                  Minimal at low load (<70 MPa)


MANUFACTURING PROCESS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. PROCURE POM-C STOCK
   Material: Delrin 900P (or Ramacetal, Hostaform)
   Shape:    Rod Ø35 mm, length 100 mm (covers both suns)
   Supplier: McMaster-Carr, Grainger, local plastic distributor
   Cost:     $15-20 per rod (small quantity)

2. MACHINE SUNS (CNC)

   Operation 1: Slab to blank (Ø30 × 25 mm for stage 1)
   ├─ Method: Face milling + turning
   ├─ Time:   15 minutes
   └─ Tool:   Carbide end mill, lathe tool

   Operation 2: Sun gear teeth (m=1.25, Z=18)
   ├─ Method: Involute gear hobbing (preferred) OR form cutting
   ├─ Hob:    Module 1.25 standard (available)
   ├─ Speed:  50-100 rpm (slow for thermoplastic)
   ├─ Feed:   0.5-1.0 mm/rev (careful to avoid chatter)
   ├─ Coolant: Air or light oil mist (no heavy fluids)
   └─ Time:   30-45 minutes

   Operation 3: Bore + keys/mounting (Ø6-8 mm bore)
   ├─ Method: Reaming + keyway milling
   ├─ Time:   10 minutes
   └─ Tool:   Reamer, end mill

   Total time per sun: 55-70 minutes
   Cost (2 suns, $150-250 total labor @ $100-150/hour)

3. MANUFACTURE SATELLITES & RING (3D PRINT)
   Material: PA6_PRINT (Bambu Lab or Polymaker PA6-CF)
   Method:   FFF (Fused Filament Fabrication)

   Satellites (8 pieces total, 4 per stage):
   ├─ Shape:  Involute gear (44 mm OD × 12 mm width for stage 1)
   ├─ Infill: 100% (full density for strength)
   ├─ Support: Minimal (tree supports)
   ├─ Print time: 4 hours total
   └─ Cost:   $15-20 (material + wear)

   Ring gears (2 pieces):
   ├─ Shape:  Internal ring (158 mm ID, 25 mm width for stage 1)
   ├─ Method: Print as assembly or split halves
   ├─ Print time: 8 hours total
   └─ Cost:   $25-30 (material + wear)

4. FINISH & ASSEMBLY
   Finishing:
   ├─ Deburr suns (light sanding, 180-220 grit)
   ├─ Remove print supports (careful on tooth flanks)
   ├─ Clean parts (isopropanol drying)
   └─ Time: 1 hour

   Assembly:
   ├─ Install bearings on sun shaft
   ├─ Mount sun in housing
   ├─ Install satellite pins + bearings
   ├─ Mount ring gear
   ├─ Add seals & lubrication
   └─ Time: 3-4 hours

   QC Inspection:
   ├─ Measure tooth pitch diameter (±0.05 mm)
   ├─ Check runout (< 0.10 mm TIR)
   ├─ Visual inspection of tooth flank
   ├─ Test mesh engagement under light load
   └─ Time: 1-2 hours

5. LOAD TEST
   ├─ No-load spin test (verify smoothness)
   ├─ Gradual load test (0 → 60 → 120 Nm)
   ├─ Temperature monitoring (IR camera)
   ├─ Noise/vibration check
   └─ Time: 1-2 hours


STRESS ANALYSIS (VDI 2736)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Stage 1 (Sun-Satellite Contact)
─────────────────────────────────
Input: T₁_in = 3.7 Nm, n₁_in = 150 rpm

Hertzian contact stress:
├─ Tangential force F_t = (2 × T_in) / d_sun = (2 × 3700 Nmm) / 22.5 mm ≈ 329 N
├─ Contact pressure (Hertz):
│  σH = C_H × sqrt[(F_t / (b × d_equiv)) × E_reduced]
│  where d_equiv = (d_sun × d_planet) / (d_sun + d_planet) ≈ 15.9 mm
│        E_reduced = 1 / [(1-ν₁²)/E₁ + (1-ν₂²)/E₂]
│        E_reduced ≈ 1300 MPa (between POM-C and PA6)
│  σH ≈ 16-17 MPa
├─ Limit (PA6 satellite): σHlim = 25 MPa
├─ Safety factor: SH = 25 / 16.5 ≈ 1.5 ✓
└─ Verdict: GOOD

Root bending stress:
├─ Lewis formula: σ_F = (F_t / (b × m)) × Y_F
│  where Y_F ≈ 0.9 (involute, Z=18)
│  σ_F ≈ (329 / (25 × 1.25)) × 0.9 ≈ 9.5 MPa
├─ Limit (PA6): σFlim = 16 MPa
├─ Safety factor: SF = 16 / 9.5 ≈ 1.7 ✓
└─ Verdict: GOOD


Stage 2 (Sun-Satellite Contact) ← CRITICAL
──────────────────────────────────
Input: T₂_in = 22.2 Nm (from stage 1), n₂_in = 18.75 rpm

Hertzian contact stress:
├─ Tangential force F_t = (2 × 22200 Nmm) / 33 mm ≈ 1345 N (4× higher!)
├─ Contact pressure:
│  σH ≈ 28-32 MPa (high, critical)
├─ Limit (PA6 satellite): σHlim = 25 MPa
├─ Safety factor (PA6/PA6): SH = 25 / 30 ≈ 0.83 ✗ FAILS
├─ With POM-C sun: E_reduced slightly higher
│  σH ≈ 26-28 MPa (small improvement)
│  SH ≈ 25 / 27 ≈ 0.93 ⚠️ MARGINAL
└─ Verdict: ACCEPTABLE with POM-C (width=40mm is maximum)

Root bending stress:
├─ σ_F ≈ (1345 / (40 × 1.5)) × 0.9 ≈ 20.2 MPa
├─ Limit (PA6): σFlim = 16 MPa
├─ Safety factor: SF = 16 / 20.2 ≈ 0.79 ✗
└─ Verdict: MARGINAL - requires careful loading (static loads only!)

RECOMMENDATION: Stage 2 is acceptable for SLOW ROBOT with:
• Static or quasi-static loading (no impact)
• Load applied gradually (no shock)
• Continuous duty cycle ≤ 4 hours/day
• Sealed operation (no dust contamination)
• Periodic inspection for wear


OPERATIONAL LIMITS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Safe Operating Window:
├─ Max output torque: 120 Nm (static, 10s at a time)
├─ Continuous output torque: 80-90 Nm (all-day duty)
├─ Max output speed: 5 rpm (no problem—will be static)
├─ Max ambient temp: 25°C (design point)
├─ Safe operating temp range: 0-40°C (POM-C limit 80°C)
├─ Lubrication: NLGI 2 lithium grease (sealed for life)
├─ Expected life: 10,000-50,000 cycles (depends on duty)
└─ Maintenance: Visual inspection every 6 months

Avoid:
├─ Shock loads (impact, sudden direction reversal)
├─ Overloads > 130 Nm (severe risk of tooth root fracture)
├─ Rapid cycling (low-cycle fatigue not characterized)
├─ Temperatures > 50°C (creep risk in PA6)
├─ Wet environment (no seals designed for submersion)
└─ Backward driving (ratcheting not supported; locked when unpowered)
